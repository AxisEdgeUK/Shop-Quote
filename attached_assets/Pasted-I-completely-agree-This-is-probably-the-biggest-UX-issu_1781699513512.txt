I completely agree.

This is probably the biggest UX issue I can see from that screenshot.

The software is behaving like a **vertical form**, when quoting is naturally a **horizontal workflow**.

Right now you've got:

* 70% white space on the right
* narrow quote cards
* constant scrolling
* pack summary isolated in a small box
* fields stacked vertically

On a 27" monitor you're only using about half the available workspace.

A machine shop estimator wants to scan information left-to-right, not top-to-bottom.

## What I would change

### Layout

Current:

```text
Customer

Part 1

Part 2

Part 3

Part 4
```

Better:

```text
---------------------------------------------------
Customer | Lead Time | Margin | Delivery
---------------------------------------------------

Part 1 | Part 2

Part 3 | Part 4

---------------------------------------------------

Pack Summary
---------------------------------------------------
```

Now 4 parts can be viewed simultaneously.

---

### Part Cards

Each part card is too tall.

Current:

```text
Part Name

Drawing Number

Revision

Material

Machine

Setup

Machining

Material Cost

Tooling
```

Make it:

```text
Part Name | Qty

Drawing | Rev

Material | Machine

Setup | Runtime

Material Cost | Tooling
```

Two-column layout.

Cuts card height almost in half.

---

### Summary Panel

The summary panel is currently wasting space.

I'd make it sticky.

```text
Customer
Margin
Lead Time

----------------

Part 1 £125

Part 2 £98

Part 3 £210

Part 4 £80

----------------

Delivery £20

Total £533
```

Always visible.

Never scrolls away.

---

### On Large Screens

Use responsive grids.

Example:

#### 2 Parts

```text
Part 1 | Part 2
```

#### 4 Parts

```text
Part 1 | Part 2

Part 3 | Part 4
```

#### 6 Parts

```text
Part 1 | Part 2

Part 3 | Part 4

Part 5 | Part 6
```

---

### Remove Dead Space

You have huge margins:

```text
| Sidebar | Quote | White Space |
```

I would use:

```text
| Sidebar | Quote Area | Summary |
```

Full width.

Much more professional.

---

### Think Like Fusion 360

The best benchmark isn't SaaS software.

It's:

* Fusion 360
* SolidWorks
* Mastercam
* Siemens NX

They all utilise screen space aggressively.

Engineers expect information density.

---
