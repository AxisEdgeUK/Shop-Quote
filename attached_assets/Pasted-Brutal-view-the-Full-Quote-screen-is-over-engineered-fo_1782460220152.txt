Brutal view: **the Full Quote screen is over-engineered for a first-time user.**

The Quick Quote is now the product’s strongest workflow. The Full Quote currently looks like someone took every useful idea and put it on one screen. That is dangerous.

## What is good

The **split drawing workspace** is excellent. Keep it.

The **summary bar on the far right** is useful. Keep it.

The **templates at the top** are useful, but they take too much premium space.

The **hidden cost reminders** are commercially smart, but visually too loud and too large.

The **Basic / Advanced toggle** is good, but Basic still looks advanced.

## What is bad

The part card is overloaded. It has too many fields visible at once:

* programming
* inspection
* deburring
* wastage
* outside process
* packaging
* delivery
* risk
* VAT
* tolerance
* surface
* complexity

That is not “Full Quote”; that is “everything quote.”

The screen feels like a control panel designed by a developer, not an estimator.

A new user will think:

> “I need to understand all this before I can send a quote.”

That is a conversion killer.

## What I would do

Make Full Quote **progressively advanced**.

Show the essential estimating fields first. Hide the rest behind expandable sections.

Default visible fields per part should be:

* Part Name
* Qty
* Drawing
* Rev
* Material
* Machine / Manual Rate
* Setup
* Machining time
* Material cost
* Tooling
* Total

Everything else should sit inside:

* Advanced Costs
* Quality & Inspection
* Notes & Risk

This is a simplification pass only.

My blunt recommendation: after this simplification, stop building and test. The Full Quote does not need more capability; it needs less visible complexity.
