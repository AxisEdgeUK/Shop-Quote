Brutally honest: it is probably **slightly too complicated for a first-time small shop user**, but it is not too complicated for the product category.

The issue is not the number of features. The issue is **when the user sees them**.

Right now, the danger is that it may feel like:

> “I need to configure a system before I can quote.”

That is bad.

What it must feel like is:

> “I can quote quickly, and the deeper tools are there when I need them.”

## My verdict

For **you**, it is useful.

For a busy 5–15 person machine shop owner seeing it for the first time, it may still feel like too much unless the default path is extremely simple.

The app needs two clear lanes:

### Lane 1: Quick Quote

For 80% of jobs.

Only show:

* customer
* part
* material
* quantity
* machine
* setup
* runtime
* margin
* delivery cost
* lead time
* PDF

### Lane 2: Full Quote

For serious quoting.

Show:

* revisions
* quantity breaks
* tolerances
* inspection
* certs
* notes
* customer terms
* internal costing

If Quick Quote is clean, the product is fine. If users land inside Full Quote first, it will feel too heavy.

## Delivery cost

Yes, add it.

Delivery is a real quoting cost and small shops often forget it.

But keep it simple:

* Delivery method
* Delivery cost
* Include in total: yes/no

Options:

* Collection
* Local delivery
* Courier
* Pallet
* Customer arranged
* Other

Do **not** build shipping integrations.

## What I would change before testing

Make Quick Quote the default button everywhere.

Full Quote should feel like:

> “advanced estimating”

not the default.
