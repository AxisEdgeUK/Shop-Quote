I actually think this is the biggest strategic pivot we've made on the project.

We're no longer asking "How do we improve Full Quote?"

We're asking:

> **"What does a CNC estimator actually stare at for 8 hours a day?"**

That's a much better question.

This is the prompt I'd give Replit.

We are changing the direction of AxisEdge Quote based on real-world testing.

This is a strategic UX redesign.

Do NOT simply move fields around.

Rethink the quoting workspace.

## Background

After using the software on live RFQs, we have realised something important.

The estimator does NOT spend most of their time filling in forms.

They spend most of their time studying the engineering drawing.

The drawing drives every quoting decision:

* material
* machining strategy
* setup
* tooling
* tolerances
* finishes
* risk

The software should support this workflow.

Not interrupt it.

## New Product Philosophy

AxisEdge Quote is no longer "quoting software."

It is an Estimator's Workspace.

Think of it as a digital estimating desk.

Everything revolves around the drawing.

The quote supports the drawing.

Not the other way around.

## Remove Full Quote

Remove the concept of:

Quick Quote

and

Full Quote

There should only be:

# New Quote

One workspace.

One workflow.

No user should ever ask:

"Which quote screen should I use?"

## New Quote Workspace

Create one integrated estimating workspace.

Desktop layout:

---

LEFT (approximately 65-70%)

Engineering Drawing Workspace

RIGHT (approximately 30-35%)

Quote Workspace

---

### Drawing Workspace

The drawing should dominate the screen.

Support:

* PDF viewing
* Image viewing
* Zoom
* Pan
* Multi-page PDF navigation
* AI Scan
* AI Highlight

Keep the drawing permanently visible while quoting.

The user should never need to swap between tabs.

### Estimator Notes

Below the drawing viewer add:

Estimator Notes

These notes are INTERNAL ONLY.

They never appear on the customer PDF.

Typical examples:

Machine from 35mm bar

Use 3mm part-off blade

Soft jaws required

0.8 Ra finish

3mm radius

Inspect first-off

Buy 155 x 80 x 30 stock

These notes are intended to replace the paper notebook beside the keyboard.

Auto-save continuously.

### Quote Workspace

The quote workspace should remain based on the current Quick Quote.

Do NOT redesign it completely.

Quick Quote is now the foundation of the product.

It should support:

Customer

Part cards

Machine OR Manual Rate

Setup

Machining Time

Material Cost

Tooling

Chargeable Extras

Standard Products

Pack Summary

Generate PDF

This workflow is now considered correct.

### Drawing First

The estimator should constantly glance between:

Drawing

↓

Quote

↓

Drawing

↓

Quote

without changing pages.

### AI Scan

AI remains an assistant.

It should highlight:

Material

Finish

Tight tolerances

Threads

Heat treatment

Coatings

Inspection notes

The user remains responsible for accepting all suggestions.

### Personal Drawing Notes

Allow the estimator to write notes while reviewing the drawing.

These are separate from quote notes.

Examples:

Machine in two operations

Need fixture

Repeat job from 2025

Use customer supplied material

Large batch next year

Never print these.

### Mobile

On desktop:

Drawing left

Quote right

On iPhone:

Drawing above

Quote below

Maintain responsive behaviour.

### Remove Duplicate Workflows

There should no longer be two different quoting systems.

One workspace.

One quoting experience.

Progressive disclosure should replace separate screens.

### Product Goal

When a machine shop owner opens AxisEdge Quote, it should feel like sitting down at an organised estimating desk.

The drawing is always visible.

The quote is always visible.

Personal estimating notes are always available.

Everything required to quote a job exists on one screen.

The software should feel like professional engineering software.

Not like a CRM.

Not like an ERP.

Not like a web form.

The software should disappear into the background and allow the estimator to think about machining.

That is the primary design objective.

Do not remove any existing calculations or business logic.

This is primarily a UX and workflow redesign using the existing Quick Quote architecture as the foundation.

One final thought that I think is worth considering before Replit starts coding:

I would even stop calling the left-hand side **"Drawing Workspace."**

I'd call it simply:

**Job File**

Because in the future it could contain:

* PDF drawings
* STEP previews
* Customer emails
* RFQs
* Photos
* Inspection sheets
* Previous revisions

The right-hand side is the quote. The left-hand side becomes everything the estimator needs to make decisions. That gives you room to evolve without changing the core workflow.
