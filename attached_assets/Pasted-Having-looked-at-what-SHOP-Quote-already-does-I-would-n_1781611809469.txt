Having looked at what SHOP Quote already does, I would **not** implement most of that prompt right now.

The prompt is drifting towards a lightweight ERP.

Here's what I would keep, remove, and defer.

### Keep Now (High Value)

**1. Missing Cost Checklist**
This directly prevents under-quoting.

**2. Quote Follow-Up**
Huge value.
Most small shops never systematically follow up quotations.

**3. Won/Lost Analysis**
You already have some of this.
Expand it slightly.

**4. Customer Quality Defaults**
Very useful.
Material certs, FAIR, inspection reports, payment terms.

**5. Better Similar Quote Search**
This is probably one of the most valuable features in the entire system.

---

### Defer Until After Testing

**Quote vs Actual Tracker**

Good idea.

But you need:

* users
* won jobs
* completed jobs

before it becomes valuable.

Build after beta.

---

### Remove Completely (For Now)

**Buyer RFQ Portal**

Sounds attractive.

But:

* adds complexity
* adds support burden
* adds customer-facing infrastructure

Not needed for first revenue.

---

**Outside Process Supplier Library**

Useful later.

Not required to prove the business.

A simple chargeable extras system already covers 80% of the value.

---

**AI Drawing Scan Expansion**

I would actually leave this alone.

Your biggest risk is becoming:

> "AI quoting software"

instead of

> "the easiest way to quote machining work."

---

### What I Think Is Missing

One thing not in that prompt:

## Quote Turnaround KPI

Every quote should record:

* RFQ received date
* Quote sent date

Dashboard:

```text
Average Quote Turnaround

This Month:
1.8 days

Last Month:
2.4 days
```

This is something owners actually care about.

---

## My Final Prompt

Review SHOP Quote and add only the highest-value features before beta testing.

Do not add ERP functionality.

Do not add stock control.

Do not add scheduling.

Do not add accounting features.

Keep the software focused on quoting.

1. Missing Cost Checklist

Before generating a customer PDF, display:

Quote Review Checklist

* Material included
* Setup included
* Programming included
* Machining time included
* Inspection included
* Tooling/NRE included
* Deburring included
* Finishing included
* Outside process included
* Delivery included
* Margin reviewed
* Quantity breaks reviewed
* Drawing revision checked

If items remain unticked:
show warning only.

Do not block PDF generation.

2. Quote Follow-Up

Add:

* Follow-up date
* Follow-up notes
* Last contacted date
* Next action

Add quick actions:

* Call Customer
* Send Follow-Up
* Waiting Response
* Won
* Lost

3. Won/Lost Analysis

Expand existing quote status reporting.

Track:

* Won date
* Lost date
* Lost reason
* Customer feedback

Lost reasons:

* Price too high
* Lead time too long
* No response
* Existing supplier
* Capability issue
* Quality requirement issue
* Other

Dashboard:

* Quotes sent
* Quotes won
* Quotes lost
* Win rate
* Total quoted value
* Total won value

4. Customer Quality Defaults

Allow customer defaults:

* Material Cert Required
* Inspection Report Required
* FAIR Required
* Certificate of Conformity Required
* Special Packaging Required
* Payment Terms
* Typical Margin
* Notes

When customer is selected:
auto-populate these defaults.

5. Similar Quote Search

Improve quote search.

Allow search by:

* Customer
* Material
* Drawing Number
* Part Name
* Quote Number
* Process
* Quantity Range

Goal:

Have we quoted this before?

Improve duplicate / quote similar workflow.

6. Quote Turnaround KPI

Track:

* RFQ Received Date
* Quote Sent Date

Dashboard:

Average Quote Turnaround

This Month
Last Month

7. Product Rule

Do not add:

* ERP features
* Stock control
* Scheduling
* Shop floor control
* Inventory management
* Purchasing systems

The goal is to remain the fastest and simplest quoting system for small CNC machine shops.

After implementation:

Run full quote flow testing and confirm nothing has broken.

If it were my money, I'd stop after that and start getting it in front of shops. The next meaningful improvements should come from real users, not us guessing.
